why getnice?

About us

On a cool summer night in 2012, getnice co-founders Barb Belsito and Jayson Carpenter met randomly at a bus stop in San Francisco. They started chatting and quickly discovered their shared love of Japan, travel, and volunteering.

In 2002, Barb raised money to go to Japan to live with a host family and volunteer with a local English club. She created a website to share her experience with friends, family, and donors. This experience opened the door to a lifelong relationship with Japan. After college, she returned to Japan to teach English through the JET Program in 2008. These days she lives and works in San Francisco, CA as a UX designer - working on getnice in her spare time.

In March 2011, shortly after the Tohoku earthquake and tsunami, professional photographer Jayson Carpenter decided to raise money to go volunteer with the relief efforts in Ishinomaki, Japan – one of the most seriously affected areas. It was through this experience that he discovered the opportunity for getnice – a platform dedicated to helping volunteers raise money while bridging the gap between volunteers and donors.

Barb and Jayson eventually partnered in 2014 to create getnice. We hope this platform will enable people to volunteer without financial limitations and allow donors to see exactly where their money is going. While Japan holds a special place in our hearts, we are excited to empower people to volunteer all over the world. We believe that the experience of giving – whether it's time or money – builds character and creates human connections in a way that nothing else can.
